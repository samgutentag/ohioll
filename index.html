Who I Am

What I Do: I provide flat-fee legal services to Ohio liquor permit holders and applicants statewide — including citation defense, permit applications and transfers, tax issues, and lease negotiations. If you hold or need an Ohio liquor permit, I can help.

I have been involved in the liquor permit industry as a bartender, manager, security person, club owner, restaurant owner and consultant since 1983 — including 13 years as owner/operator of a high-traffic club across the street from The Ohio State University campus and a restaurant in Powell, Ohio. I have experienced first hand many of the same issues Ohio liquor permit holders and applicants face today.

Since earning my law degree from Capital University, I have focused my practice on Ohio liquor law. I combine the knowledge I have collected working in the industry with my more than thirty years as an attorney to assist my clients through the many complex licensing, application, tax, citation and other issues associated with a liquor permit in Ohio.

Today I represent individuals, small businesses and major corporate chains with liquor permits in every corner of the state of Ohio on all matters related to their liquor permits, from application process to renewals to citations to liquor permit transfers.

Having been a liquor permit holder myself, I know the importance of your bottom line.

That is why I provide a majority of liquor related services such as routine liquor citations, appeals, permit transfer applications and new permit applications for a flat-fee agreed to by you and me ahead of time. No hourly rates. No hidden charges or costs.

Flat-Fee Pricing

As a former restaurant and bar owner, I know the importance of keeping costs in line. That is why a majority of my legal services are provided on a flat-fee, a fee agreed to by you and me ahead of time.

There are no hidden fees or costs.

You never pay extra for my drive time to or within Columbus. My office is in the heart of downtown Columbus, just two blocks from the Ohio Liquor Commission and minutes away from the Ohio Division of Liquor Control. It is also near the Franklin County, Ohio Courts, where all appeals from the decisions of the Ohio Liquor Commission are heard. Also, you never pay extra for time spent waiting at the Ohio Liquor Commission for your case to be heard. It is included in the flat fee.

To me, part of being a good lawyer is being cost-effective for my clients. Legal expertise at a fair, honest price — that is what I can provide as your Ohio Liquor Lawyer.
