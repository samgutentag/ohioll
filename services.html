Services

Services for Current Permit Holders

Services for current Ohio liquor permit holders include, but are not limited to, the following areas:

Legal Issues

  • I defend clients against any violation citation before the Ohio Liquor Control Commission.
  • I appear before the Franklin County Court of Common Pleas on behalf of clients facing suspension or revocation of their liquor permit by the Ohio Liquor Control Commission.
  • I appear before the 10th District Court of Appeals on behalf of clients facing suspension or revocation of their liquor permit by the Court of Common Pleas (affirming an order by the Ohio Liquor Control Commission).
  • I defend clients on objections to the renewal of their liquor permit before the Ohio Division of Liquor Control and Ohio Liquor Control Commission.

Tax Issues

  • I negotiate and appear before the Ohio Department of Taxation to resolve permit holders' sales tax and withholding tax issues.
  • I negotiate and appear before the Ohio Bureau of Worker's Compensation on clients' worker's compensation tax issues.
  • I negotiate and appear before the Ohio Attorney General's Office on sales tax collection issues.
  • I appear before municipal and common pleas courts to defend employees and owners charged criminally with violations of the Ohio Revised Code.

Services for New Permit Applicants

Services for new Ohio liquor permit applicants:

Document Preparation

I will prepare all documents necessary to apply for a new Ohio Liquor Permit, including:

  • Liquor Permit Application
  • Corporate Disclosure forms
  • Personal History forms
  • Tenancy forms
  • Source of Funds form
  • All other required documents

Filing & Monitoring

I will also assemble and file your application documents with the Ohio Division of Liquor Control, and then:

  • Monitor the processing of your application by the state, reviewing and responding to all status reports from the Division of Liquor Control.
  • Monitor local government review of the application.
  • Represent you at any hearings on any objections to the issuance of your liquor permit.
  • Represent you at any hearing denying the issuance of your liquor permit.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I receive a liquor citation in Ohio?

If you receive a citation from the Ohio Liquor Control Commission, you have the right to a hearing. I defend clients against all types of violation citations before the Commission. If the outcome is unfavorable, I can appeal to the Franklin County Court of Common Pleas and beyond. Most citation defense is handled for a flat fee agreed to in advance.

How much do your legal services cost?

A majority of my liquor-related legal services are provided for a flat fee agreed to by you and me ahead of time. There are no hourly rates, no hidden charges, and no extra cost for drive time to Columbus or time spent waiting at the Ohio Liquor Commission. Call me for a free consultation to discuss your specific situation.

How do I apply for a new Ohio liquor permit?

Applying for a new Ohio liquor permit requires preparing and filing multiple documents with the Ohio Division of Liquor Control, including the permit application, corporate disclosure forms, personal history forms, tenancy forms, and source of funds documentation. I handle the entire process for a flat fee — from document preparation through filing, monitoring, and representing you at any hearings.

How does a liquor permit transfer work in Ohio?

A liquor permit transfer involves negotiating and preparing asset purchase agreements, completing all required application documents, filing with the Ohio Division of Liquor Control, and monitoring the application through approval. I handle the complete transfer process for a flat fee, including representation at any hearings on objections or denials.

Do you handle tax issues related to liquor permits?

Yes. I negotiate and appear before the Ohio Department of Taxation on sales tax and withholding tax issues, before the Ohio Bureau of Worker's Compensation on worker's compensation tax issues, and before the Ohio Attorney General's Office on sales tax collection issues. I also defend employees and owners charged with criminal violations of the Ohio Revised Code.
